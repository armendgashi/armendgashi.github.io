Research

Special Token Injection (STI)

2025 to 2026 · Completed

Introduced STI as a new, distinct vulnerability category targeting how LLM inference providers and agentic applications handle special / control tokens. Work spanned theoretical framing, validation across providers, public CWE submission, and talks at DEF CON 33 and multiple BSides. A formal research paper is in progress, scheduled for submission in Q4 2026.

Evaluations for offensive security agents Ongoing

2026 to Present

Building an LLM-as-judge harness for grading offensive security agent trajectories against human-authored rubrics, retargeted to AppSec (web apps, APIs, auth flows, injection classes). Pairs a deterministic coverage layer (no LLM) with per-leaf LLM judges, produces F1 against human ground truth, cost-vs-accuracy Pareto frontiers, and RL / fine-tuning exports. Built on the methodology of the PentestJudge paper (Caldwell et al., dreadnode, 2025).

SakiX

Research · 2024

Early research on what AI could do for offensive security: a multi-agent CLI that runs Semgrep, triages findings with an LLM to cut false positives, and drives a separate agent to read code and propose PoCs. Not a product, a hands-on prototype to see how far agentic workflows could go on real code.
